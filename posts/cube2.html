Toy Puzzle (2025-03-29)

Part One

Picking up the pieces

Picking up where we stopped last time, magnetic speedcubes were a premium back then. Initially being exclusive to sponsored world class cubers, then trickling down to cubing stores selling cubes modded with magnets, until finally cubing companies started selling their magnetic cubes en masse. However, the pricing of these cubes were a fortune to say the least.

It wasn't until YJ came out with their newest magnetised release, the YJ MGC 3x3 puzzle that I became interested in getting one for myself. Their offering costed a generous amount compared to the competition. But if I wanted a magnetic cube one day, I'm gonna need to set a better time than subpar sub-50 times. It was either the MGC or the GTS2 M.

Car Puzzle

Oh yeah, I got myself a Moyu Weilong GTS2 not long after getting the Gan puzzle. I still have both of those cubes in my collection. The only one missing is my MF3RS unfortunately. But if I had to compare between the three cubes, the Weilong feels far superior compared to the Gan. I could be biased though since my first speedcube is a Moyu speedcube.

The Weilong feels like a more premium MF3RS, though I prefer the MF3RS more. I would describe the MF3RS as having a lighter "Weilong" feel. By this point, my times have gotten negligibly quicker. But then, I stopped.

6 years

I never had gotten one magnetic speedcube, because I stopped speedcubing abruptly. I was being bombarded with schoolwork, is all I could say. Another part of me believed I stopped because it was getting "mainstream", but I think it's just nonsense to me as the community is still as positive as ever.

I recently returned to speedcubing because someone had brought in their own speedcube to toy with (I believe it was a QiYi M Pro 3x3), I got surprised hearing that magnetic puzzles have gotten cheaper over the years. Now I searched for Gan cubes (for some reason) and assumed it would have gotten cheaper, right? Wrong, they still cost as pricey as ever. But that's for their latest release. Their older ones would cost a lot cheaper too, right? You guessed it, it's still pricey compared to what other brands are offering.

To sticker, or not to sticker

One cube that has picqued my interest is the Qiyi XMD Tornado V3, I was rather dumbfounded by the different versions it had. I was confused between whatever surface options they offered. I mean, UV coating? That sounded like a ridiculous type of material to put on a puzzle. As this was my first time buying a stickerless puzzle, (I had a stickerless Qiyi Pyraminx, but that's only because there was no stickered version up for sale) since I quit speedcubing before the WCA* made stickerless puzzles legal in the regulations. It was sort of odd seeing no puzzles sold in a stickered variant on the market.

I kind of miss the looks of a stickered puzzle. It's classic, but the cons outweigh the pros of it, because stickered puzzles could wear out compared to stickerless ones. This convinced me to get a stickerless one, not that I have any other options anyway. Speaking of options, there are three surface options of which are:

  • Glossy - As the name suggests, the surface is smooth plastic, your fingers grip this kind of surface easily. But beware that having wet hands can make the puzzle slip out of your hands. It's still found on some cheap puzzles, like on the base model XMD Tornado 3x3 puzzles, and the Dayan Guhong Pro.
  • Matte/Frosted - I have no idea what the difference is between frosted and matte so I'll group them together. This type of surface is typically found on cheaper variants of puzzles nowadays, but it was one of two surface options between 2019 and 2021, the other being glossy surface. Gan and Moyu popularised this surface type. It usually wears down into a more glossy look the more it is used. However, some earlier stickerless puzzles back in 2017 used a frosted surface, which doesn't seem to wear down.
  • UV Coated - A new type of surface "invented" by Gan. Puzzles in this variant cost a fortune compared to their matte/glossy counterpart. An example being the HuaMeng TG. This surface feels like a glossy surface, except it's a lot shinier and it's not easily scratched. Has the benefits and disadvantages of a glossy surface but cranked up to 5. Doesn't help that some puzzles can have flaws in their UV coating application. Gan, obviously has the best UV coating application whereas some Moyu puzzles can have imperfections.

This sounds overwhelming, but to simplify, glossy/uv is good when dry hands, not good when sweaty whereas matte is good when wet hands or worn down by a lot, not good when out of the box while frosted is a no go on so many levels unless you have sweaty hands all the time.

Red pill or blue pill**

The Tornado V3 picqued my interest because a lot of people say it feels so good and has adjustable magnet strengths. I went in blindly and bought the CHEAPEST variant of the bunch, which do not include the corner piece to core magnets. Now not to bash the puzzle, it is actually REALLY REALLY good compared to my other ones. It felt like a good deal, but I couldn't claim it to be the best puzzle of all time since this was the only modern 3x3 puzzle that I have and I couldn't compare it to anything else.

And so I bought a Moyu RS3M V5 for really cheap. It has a matte surface compared to the Tomato V3's glossy surface. It started out very slippery to hold but after many solves, it wore down to be slightly shinier and grippier than before. At this point, my house might have one too many twisty puzzles. But I needed the expand my collection with quality puzzles, and not cheap dollar store puzzles. I got rid of most of the cheap puzzles since theres no point in keeping those and I only have sentimental value for one of those.

Moyu MFJSMF3RS3M2025 this section feels like a shitpost

I like the RS3M V5 more than my Tornado V3, which is ironic but not surprising given that the Tomato is a more expensive puzzle than the aforementioned puzzle.

I couldn't get this feeling of FOMO out of my head, by not getting the version of the Tomato which included the corner to core magnets. I had to do something about it, I had to. And so I began a j
